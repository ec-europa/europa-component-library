  The footer is present on every page under ec.europa.eu. It provides

## Anatomy

| Name             | Constraint | Restrictions                                                                                   |
| ---------------- | ---------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Corporate footer | Mandatory  |                                                                                                |
| Custom footer    | Optional   | If the corporate footer doesn't meet the site requirements, custom content can be added on top |

The footer appears at the bottom of every ec.europa.eu pages.

### Corporate footer

It is divided into 2 functional areas:

- The main content area serves as an extra entry point to the most important sections of the site, social media platforms and other EU institutions.
- The secondary content area is reserved for information concerning the Commission's web presence, resources for partners, cookies & privacy, legal notices and contact information.

![Corporate footer](https://inno-ecl.s3.amazonaws.com/media/images/EC/Footer%20EC/corporate_footer.png)

### Custom footer

The custom footer is an exception, it's only used when the corporate footer areas do not satisfy the requirements of the specific DG, project, campaign, etc.

The custom footer can contain:

- identification of the site name
- information on connecting to social platforms
- contact information
- other relevant information, such as a link to the site map

![Custom footer](https://inno-ecl.s3.amazonaws.com/media/images/EC/Footer%20EC/customer_footer.png)

## When to use

- Always, at the bottom of every page of the European Commission's websites.
